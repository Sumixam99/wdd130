Client Report

Voices of the Book of Mormon

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

It took a while to find pictures. It was hard to find any images that I could use for free, but, after a lot of searching, I came across a website that had a whole bunch of paintings of Book of Mormon figures that said the images could be used for non-commercial purposes. I used those images.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

Me and Sadie mostly communicated while in class. After she finished the page, she reached out to me over email let me know that it was finished. There weren't any problems with communication.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Sadie did a great job, the website turned out well. I like that one the chapter pages the key always stays on screen.

What will you do differently next time you hire a developer to do a project for you?

Next time I would like to have more resources and a more thorough plan for the website.

Developer Report

Outbound

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

Me and Porter communicated in class, I showed him the website a few times as I was working on it so he could know how it was progressing.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

When I finished the website, I had Porter take a look at it, after I got his approval, I sent him a ZIP file containing his website.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

It was easy to manage the website on GitHub, I developed the site and used live server to check it, then when I needed to make a progress report I pushed it all to GitHub which was super easy to do.

General

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part for me was finding pictures that could be used for my site, it was a pain to do.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

My favorite part was adding the paralaxing background to the website I developed. I think it turned out nicely and it's neat.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

If I made the website again, I would want to make it in a way that the code is much cleaner, I'm pretty sure I repeated myself in the CSS file which made everything messier and harder to figure out.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

I feel more confident in my ability to solve problems that come up in developing a website.
